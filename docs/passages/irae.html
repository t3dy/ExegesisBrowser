The Pantocrator judges the world. But first, before the dies irae occurs, Elijah is sent here to change the world, to make it acceptable before the judgment comes. Thus Elijah not only comes (first) but also changes the world into something that will be judged favorably - so that not a curse - God's curse - but God's approbation, will later occur. This does not mean that at the "Elijah stage" the world will be made perfect (i.e. the kingdom established); he will merely make it good enough - tilt the scales a la Ma'at's weighing. So at the time in which Elijah returns + does his work there will be crucial but not complete transformation; he, + what he achieves, is (only) the necessary forerunner. + it is said "he will come with the sun with healing in its wings" - it will be a sort of healing - but if one wishes to appraise the sort of changes Elijah would tend to make, consider what he did vis-a-vis king Ahab.

In Malachi it's stated that "on that day" (when God acts) * "the sun of righteousness shall rise with healing in his wings, + you shall break loose like calves released from the stall. On the day that I act, you shall trample down the wicked, for they will be ashes under the soles of your feet, says the Lord of Hosts... Look, I will send you the prophet Elijah before (sic) the great + terrible day of the Lord comes ...lest I come + put the land under a ban to destroy it."

This is not the Parousia (track C); this (the above underlined) would be the events of 1974. USA. Portugal. Greece. Spain.
