What is obvious, so obvious that one overlooks it, is that the Logos qua Jesus was bound by the limitations and laws binding all men, and could not do anything "cosmic," either for his friends or for himself. It is equally obvious that the "Second Comforter" is another form of himself, but, being released by the above laws and limitations, could render aid on an unlimited basis, in ways that are transcendent and supernatural, and not the mere "magician's tricks" which Jesus was required to do, merely to show his divinity. What I see now, from reading "The Wisdom of Solomon" is simply that St. Sophia entered human form and during this time lost the various capacities (or some of them) which she had possessed as a world-builder and maintainer; it was essential that His/Her time incarnate be brief, which is exactly what Jesus says to his descriples, comparing his pain to that of childbirth. So there was Santa Sophia ranging the world, then limited to the human form, then released after the crucifixion -- to become what? There is mention in "The Wisdom of Solomon" of St. Sophia coming to good and holy men and inspiring them such as to make clear an evident similarity if not identity between the Holy Spirit (and receiving the Holy Spirit) and receiving St. Sophia. But there may be a difference. St. Sophia, prior to the incarnation, may, as is said, have been limited to coming to very special certain men, a very few saints and holy men, excluding the mass of mankind. Once, however, she had enduring and entered earthly life as we men do, then there would be no circumscription as above; she would be able, as the Holy Spirit, to range within every human soul.

The crucifixion and resurrection was a demonstration of exactly what the writer (first century B.C.) claimed for Saint Sophia, that she conferred immortality. The Logos or St. Sophia or the Holy Spirit is the key to immortality, but that is only a means (i.e. immortality) the end being to bring man closer to God. This is almost universally overlooked. Where are we going to live while immortal? In Southern California? What is meant by "immortality" must be, as Henry Vaughn figured, "to life somehow with or within or a part of God; there can be no figures in white robes; those are messengers, not departed spirits. What the whole thing is about is to draw man back into union with God; that is the ultimate goal. The means required the incarnation, because although Santa Sophia could temporarily inspire certain men, evidently she was a far-ranging plasmatic demiurge which could not be bonded to what are called "fallen" or "sinful" men. It is precisely these men whom Christ lived with, and who became available to the post-incarnation spirit which formerly (according to my theory) could not suffuse or infuse anyone but special --which is to say, a very few-- men. Once incarnated, the mystic body of her/him/it could transubstantiate this entire material world, and us as portions of it, making use of all substances here, without regard to their special piety (see Luther, on Christianity as the religion of the unfit, the unworthy, and the very damned.").
