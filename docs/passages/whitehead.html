My situation vis-a-vis the Xerox missive can therefore be construed as a binary choice, an either-or choosing by the Wise Mind, the totality of the system. This choosing, this act of choice, is (it would seem) its essential action; this is what it does; this is God as Creator. Behind the choice lie intelligence (wisdom), power (the ability to act) and love (the desire to act). By its wisdom it knows which fork to choose; by its power it executes the choice; and its love motivates it to act. Power, wisdom, love. Showing up in a binary fork either-or off - on selection, "selection of the good," as Whitehead put it. This, then, is Whitehead's process (dynamic) deity. The deity, the system, chose for me. I did not choose; it chose. What it did is normal. That I know about it -- that is not. So the purpose of the theophany is an example of God in relationship to the universe continually. There was nothing miraculous in it as an event; only my being aware of it is, in a certain real way, miraculous. In no way (I mean) was it a suspension of natural law. This instance is how all change occurs, but normally we do not see or understand. Deity did not overrule reality; deity is reality. Deity as reality perpetually makes such branching choices. I must conclude, therefore, that it wanted me to know. I have read (the E.B.?) that this is regarded by theologians as the real purpose of miracle: to display and reveal God's power, and, of course, his love... that is, God Himself is revealed by the act. This, too, is normal; again: the only unusual part is my perception of it. It tried out letting me handle the Xerox missive on my own, then it rejected that and, in place, tried out the binary alternative, which I experienced as being overridden.
